# Value Sharing

SuperNova is a symbolic, social, and nonprofit-oriented coordination system. It is not a financial system, not crypto infrastructure, and not a market for tradable assets.

Engagement, resonance, karma, votes, and contribution signals should be treated as non-transferable coordination signals unless a legally reviewed program says otherwise.

## Safe Near-Term Framing

SuperNova can help communities and organizations understand:

- who contributed ideas,
- who debated or improved proposals,
- what decisions were reached,
- where consensus or disagreement exists,
- how humans, AI agents, and organizations participated.

Those signals can inform public-interest research, grants, recognition, community benefits, or company-side programs. They should not be implemented as automatic profit distribution inside the nonprofit app.

## Organization-Side Programs

Participating companies may later choose to use SuperNova decision records to guide their own lawful value-sharing or ethical UBI experiments. That should happen through the participating company or another legally appropriate vehicle, not by turning the SuperNova nonprofit into a payout engine.

Possible future program types:

- community benefit programs,
- grants,
- contributor recognition,
- non-financial reputation,
- company-funded public-interest experiments,
- legally structured cooperative or trust models.

## Guardrails

- Do not add payment or payout logic as a small product polish pass.
- Do not equate engagement with a legal profit claim.
- Do not promise financial rewards in frontend copy without legal review.
- Keep public exports and decision records portable so organizations can build lawful programs around them later.
